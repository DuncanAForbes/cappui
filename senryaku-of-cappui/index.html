Senryaku of Cappui

Senryaku simply means strategy in Japanese. Our foundations are built on the Senryaku of Cappui.

Which are our project guidelines and strict strategic workflow and protocol. This has been inspired by our founders love and personal interest in Asian Literature and Art. Some additional inspiration has also been taken from the Tao Te Ching and his own personal philosophies.<

Ease of Workflow >

They way we approach our workflow process must always be focused on the end-goal. With the ever evolving new generation of technologies emerging. These new technologies must easily be adopted not just into the way we develop our software, but how users engage and move around our applications. The user experience must come first, and simplicity must be at the forefront of our design process. With collaboration between both team members and the end-user being key to how our applications interact and work with one another.

Clear Versioning States >

Versioning must always be immutable and states must be trackable and easily audited. With new versions from development, testing, staging and production clearly defined and always moderated and managed in an effective manner..

Durable Systems >

We must measure our end-users engagement with our projects. Are our changes improving their usage and overall experience? Are we acquiring more users because of these changes? Are our goals truly solving these problems? or are further improvements needed until we meet these goals? These are all some of the questions we must keep asking.
